The New 7 Wonders of the World


Great Wall of China (China): A massive 21,196 km series of fortifications built over centuries to protect the northern border.

Petra (Jordan): An ancient city carved directly into vibrant pink sandstone cliffs.

The Colosseum (Italy): The largest ancient amphitheatre ever built, once hosting 50,000 spectators for gladiatorial games.

Chichén Itzá (Mexico): A Mayan city featuring the El Castillo pyramid, which has 365 steps—one for each day of the year.

Machu Picchu (Peru): A 15th-century Incan citadel built high in the Andes Mountains using sophisticated dry-stone masonry.

Taj Mahal (India): An ivory-white marble mausoleum built by Emperor Shah Jahan as a monument of love for his wife.

Christ the Redeemer (Brazil): A 98-foot tall Art Deco statue of Jesus overlooking Rio de Janeiro from Mount Corcovado.

Great Pyramid of Giza (Egypt): While not a "new" winner, it was granted honorary status as the only surviving member of the ancient wonders.
